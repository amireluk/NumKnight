### Knight (Player)

```
A single flat image showing exactly 4 poses of the same character, one in each corner of the image. This is knight character. The character appears exactly ONCE per corner — one pose per corner, four corners total. Same knight. Corner positions: top-left corner = Idle pose, top-right corner = Attack pose, bottom-left corner = Hit pose, bottom-right corner = Dead pose. Exactly one pose per corner, no duplicates. Style (applies to ALL 4 cells): Flat 2D cartoon game sprite. VERY THICK, VERY BOLD black outlines on every character edge — heavy enough for clean chroma-key or edge-detection removal. All colour effects, glows, and magic must be fully enclosed within the thick black outlines — absolutely no glow, bloom, or light bleed outside the outlines. Solid flat colours only — no shading, no gradients, no lighting, no drop shadows, no cast shadows, no self-shadowing, no textures, no 3D effects. Cute, heroic, and friendly — this is the PLAYER character, a brave little champion the child is rooting for. He should radiate cheerful determination and heroic energy, not menace. Suitable for children ages 5–10. Rounded shapes, small neat confident eyes visible through the visor — proportionate, not oversized bug-eyes. Warm friendly face. Art style similar to Clash Royale or Angry Birds characters. BACKGROUND COLOUR — CRITICAL: The entire image background is ONE single flat colour — magenta (#FF00FF) — identical across all 4 sections. Every background pixel in the whole image is the exact same magenta. Do not vary the background shade, tint, or hue between sections. Pure flat fill, no gradients, no noise, no vignette, no variation. Magenta was chosen because it does not appear anywhere in the character's colour scheme. The knight wears silver-blue rounded plate armour, gold trim, red plume on the helmet — he looks like a proud little hero, not a villain. CRITICAL — consistent canvas: In all 4 cells the knight must be the same size and the feet must be at the same vertical position so that swapping between sprites looks seamless in a game engine. The 4 poses (all facing RIGHT): Top-left — Idle: Relaxed combat stance. Sword held down at his left side, round shield on right arm raised slightly. Feet planted, weight on back foot. Calm and ready. Top-right — Attack: Mid-swing lunge. Sword arm fully extended to the right, sword pointing forward, body weight on front foot, leaning into the strike. Explosive dynamic pose. Bottom-left — Hit (taking damage): Recoiling backwards to the left. Body leaning back, shield raised high defensively, free arm out for balance, slightly hunched and off-balance. Bottom-right — Dead: Collapsed on the ground. Lying flat or slumped sideways, sword dropped, shield fallen, eyes closed. Fully defeated. The background colour fills the entire image continuously — there are absolutely no lines, borders, dividers, or any visual separation anywhere in the image. The four poses share one seamless flat background. Each pose must be fully contained within its corner area — no body part, weapon, or effect may extend into another corner. No labels, no text, no watermarks. The bottom 250 pixels of the image must be solid black — no character content in that strip. This black area will be cropped in post-processing. No background scenery, no floor, no ground plane, no shadows of any kind — no drop shadows, no cast shadows, no self-shadowing, no photorealism, no 3D render look, no HDR glow.
```
